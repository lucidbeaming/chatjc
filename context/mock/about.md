# About Anon

## Current Focus

Anon is currently based in Portland, OR. They are looking for a mid-level backend developer role at a startup or mid-sized company. They prefer remote work but are open to hybrid arrangements in the Portland metro area.

Right now Anon is building a recipe management app in Vue.js and experimenting with fine-tuning small language models for text classification. They recently completed an online course in Rust and are exploring WebAssembly for performance-critical browser applications.

## Career Path

Anon started programming in high school, building simple games in Python. They studied Computer Science at a state university and got their first job as a QA engineer at a logistics company. After two years of writing test scripts, they moved into backend development and haven't looked back.

The thread connecting their career is problem-solving. Whether debugging a flaky integration test or designing a data pipeline, Anon enjoys breaking complex systems into understandable pieces.

Anon is largely self-taught beyond their CS degree. They credit open source projects and technical blogs for most of their practical knowledge.

## Technical Approach

Anon's preferred stack is Python or Go for backends, PostgreSQL for data, and Vue.js when frontend work is needed. They value simplicity and readability over cleverness.

They believe documentation is an underrated skill and try to write clear READMEs and inline comments for every project they ship.

## Working Style

Anon is a quiet but effective collaborator. They prefer async communication and written proposals over long meetings. They have mentored two junior developers at previous jobs and found it rewarding.
